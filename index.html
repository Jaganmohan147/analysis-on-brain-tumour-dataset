About Project

Abstract

Brain tumors are abnormal growths in
the brain that can be benign or malignant. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment and survival rates. This project analyzes a Brain
Tumor Dataset using Pandas Profiling to explore patterns in tumor types, patient demographics, and MRI scan features. The goal is to identify
key risk factors and assist in diagnosis using data analysis. 

Introduction

Brain tumors affect thousands of people worldwide. Medical imaging (MRI scans) plays a vital role in detecting and classifying tumors. By
analyzing patient data and tumor characteristics, we can understand patterns in tumor occurrence and severity.

Literature Survey

Research shows that age, genetics, and environmental factors contribute to brain tumor development. Studies also highlight that machine learning models can classify tumors based on MRI images. Data analysis helps in identifying tumor growth patterns, risk factors, and survival
probabilities.

EDA

EDA helps in understanding tumor trends. Pandas Profiling is used to analyze:
• Tumor types – Identifying common tumor types (benign, malignant, glioma, meningioma).
• Age & tumor risk – Checking if older individuals have a higher tumor occurrence.
• MRI scan features – Analyzing pixel intensity and size of detected tumors.
• Symptoms & tumor severity – Understanding how symptoms correlate with tumor type.
• Survival rates – Checking survival trends based on tumor type and treatment.
• Correlations – Finding relationships between patient age, tumor size, and risk factors.
